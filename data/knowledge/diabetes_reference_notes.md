# Diabetes Follow-up and Documentation Notes (RAG)

## Quick clinical grounding
- Diabetes follow-up notes should capture glycemic control trends, medication adherence, diet/activity context, and symptom burden.
- Hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia symptom history should be documented when present.
- Long-term risk reduction includes blood pressure, lipid, kidney, eye, and foot care monitoring.

## Documentation cues for notes
- Include patient-reported home glucose patterns when available.
- Record antidiabetic medications with timing and reported tolerance.
- Document symptoms suggestive of hypo/hyperglycemia (for example dizziness, sweating, confusion, polyuria, polydipsia).
- Include lifestyle context: dietary adherence, physical activity, and barriers.
- Add follow-up interval and clear return precautions for severe symptoms.

## Practical plan anchors
- Reinforce medication adherence and self-monitoring instructions.
- Encourage individualized nutrition and activity goals.
- Recommend timely follow-up for persistent uncontrolled readings or symptomatic episodes.
