# Asthma and COPD Documentation Notes (RAG)

## Quick clinical grounding
- Respiratory chronic disease notes should distinguish baseline status from exacerbation features.
- Symptom profile should include breathlessness pattern, cough, wheeze, sputum changes, and trigger exposure.
- Exacerbation risk increases with poor medication adherence, infection, smoke exposure, and prior severe episodes.

## Documentation cues for notes
- Capture symptom onset, progression, and impact on activity/sleep.
- Record current inhaler/medication use and technique/adherence concerns if mentioned.
- Include objective indicators when available (respiratory rate, oxygen saturation, accessory muscle use).
- Document trigger history: allergens, smoke, pollution, infection, weather, exertion.
- Specify action plan and escalation criteria for worsening dyspnea or low oxygen signs.

## Practical plan anchors
- Continue/optimize controller and rescue strategy per existing clinician regimen.
- Counsel trigger avoidance and inhaler technique reinforcement.
- Provide emergency warning signs and follow-up timeframe.
