# Fever Clinical Notes (RAG)

## Quick clinical grounding
- Fever is a sign, not a diagnosis; it often reflects infectious or inflammatory processes.
- Normal temperature varies by person and method; interpretation should include measurement context.
- Most common causes are infections, but medication effects, heat illness, autoimmune conditions, and malignancy are possible contributors.

## Documentation cues for notes
- Record duration and pattern of fever, maximum measured temperature, and measurement route when known.
- Include associated symptoms (for example cough, sore throat, dyspnea, headache, nausea, urinary symptoms).
- Document hydration status and oral intake.
- Record medications already used (for example acetaminophen or ibuprofen) and response.
- Capture red-flag symptoms and provide explicit return precautions.

## Practical care reminders
- Encourage hydration and symptom monitoring.
- Antipyretic use can be documented when clinically appropriate.
- Avoid inventing medication dose/frequency when audio text is uncertain; mark as unspecified and recommend confirmation.
