# Respiratory Symptom Triage Notes (RAG)

## Quick clinical grounding
- For fever/cough presentations, clinical notes should separate symptom history from objective findings.
- Useful subjective details include onset, duration, progression, sputum or dry cough, dyspnea, chest pain, and sick contacts when available in conversation.
- Objective elements should include available vitals (temperature, pulse, oxygen saturation, respiratory rate) and exam findings if present.

## Documentation cues for SOAP
- Subjective: patient-reported timeline and associated symptoms.
- Objective: measurable findings and any observed respiratory distress signs.
- Assessment: likely syndrome based on available evidence, clearly marked as provisional if uncertainty remains.
- Plan: hydration/supportive care, medication continuation instructions, monitoring interval, and escalation criteria.

## Escalation red flags to capture
- Worsening shortness of breath
- Chest pain
- Confusion or altered mental status
- Persistent high fever or clinical deterioration
