# Medication Safety and Reconciliation Notes (RAG)

## Quick clinical grounding
- Medication safety documentation should prioritize accurate medication name, dose, route, and frequency.
- Source reliability matters: conversation-derived medication details may be incomplete and require confirmation.
- Polypharmacy, duplicate classes, and high-risk combinations increase adverse event risk.

## Documentation cues for notes
- List each medication with known details; explicitly mark unknown dose/frequency as unspecified.
- Capture patient-reported side effects and adherence barriers.
- Note allergies or prior adverse drug reactions if provided.
- Distinguish current meds from recently stopped medications.
- Document counseling points and monitoring recommendations.

## Practical plan anchors
- Perform medication reconciliation at each follow-up when feasible.
- Avoid inventing doses from unclear audio transcript text.
- Use clear patient-facing instructions for timing and warning signs.
