Clinical Documentation Quick Guide

Fever and cough triage:
For uncomplicated fever and cough, document duration, associated symptoms, red flags, and hydration status.
Common plan elements include hydration advice, antipyretics if appropriate, and follow-up if symptoms persist or worsen.

Hypertension follow-up:
Document medication adherence, home blood pressure trends, adverse effects, and lifestyle factors.
Typical plan elements include blood pressure monitoring, diet and exercise counseling, and medication adjustment only with clear indication.

Medication documentation:
Always record medicine name, dose if available, frequency, and patient-reported response.
When unsure about dose from conversation audio, mark as unspecified rather than inventing values.

SOAP quality reminders:
Subjective should reflect patient-reported history.
Objective should include measurable findings when present.
Assessment should state the most likely clinical impression from available data.
Plan should be actionable, concise, and aligned with findings.
